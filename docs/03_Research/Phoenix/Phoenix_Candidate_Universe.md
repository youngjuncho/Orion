# Purpose

This document defines the candidate universe used by Phoenix.

Phoenix does not evaluate the entire digital asset market.

Instead, Phoenix evaluates a curated set of candidate assets within each approved category.

Leader selection occurs only within the candidate universe.

---

# Design Philosophy

Phoenix evaluates:

Category

↓

Candidate Universe

↓

Leader

↓

Challenger

↓

Replacement Risk

---

# Candidate Inclusion Criteria

Candidate assets should satisfy most of the following:

* Meaningful category relevance
* Sufficient liquidity
* Meaningful market capitalization
* Active ecosystem participation
* Reliable data availability

# Candidate Addition Rules

New candidates may be added if:

* Category relevance is demonstrated
* Market significance increases
* Reliable data becomes available

All additions must be documented in:

Decision_Log.md

before implementation.

---

# Candidate Removal Rules

Candidates may be removed if:

* Ecosystem relevance deteriorates
* Liquidity becomes insufficient
* Data quality becomes unreliable

---

# Relationship With Category Framework

Category Framework defines categories.

Candidate Universe defines eligible assets within categories.

Leader selection occurs only from the Candidate Universe.
