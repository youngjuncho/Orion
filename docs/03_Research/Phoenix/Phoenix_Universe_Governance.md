# Purpose

This document defines how the Phoenix Candidate Universe is governed.

The objective is to ensure candidate selection remains consistent, transparent, and reproducible.

---

# Governance Scope

This document governs:

* Candidate additions
* Candidate removals
* Category changes
* Universe reviews

---

# Governance Principles

Phoenix shall maintain:

* Category relevance
* Liquidity standards
* Data availability
* Operational simplicity

---

# Candidate Lifecycle

Candidate

↓

Approved Candidate

↓

Leader / Challenger

↓

Retired

---

# Candidate Addition Criteria

A candidate may be added if most of the following are true:

* Strong category relevance
* Meaningful ecosystem participation
* Sufficient liquidity
* Reliable market data
* Long-term viability

---

# Candidate Removal Criteria

A candidate may be removed if:

* Category relevance materially declines
* Liquidity becomes insufficient
* Reliable data is unavailable
* Project becomes inactive

---

# Category Addition Criteria

A new category may be created only if:

* Long-term relevance exists
* Multiple competitors exist
* Reliable data sources exist
* Portfolio implications are understood

---

# Category Removal Criteria

A category may be removed if:

* Economic relevance disappears
* Candidate universe collapses
* Data becomes unreliable

---

# Review Authority

Universe changes require:

* Research review
* Documentation update
* Decision Log entry

before implementation.

---

# Review Frequency

Universe Review:

Quarterly

Category Review:

Quarterly

Framework Review:

Annually

---

# Decision Logging

All material universe changes must be recorded in:

Decision_Log.md

before implementation.
