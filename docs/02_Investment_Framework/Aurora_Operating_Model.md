# Aurora Operating Model

## Purpose

This document describes the operating model of Aurora within Orion OS.

Aurora is Orion's market climate framework.

Unlike Moon, Supernova, and Phoenix, Aurora does not allocate capital.

Aurora exists to provide environmental context.

---

# Objective

Aurora exists to answer a single question:

"What environment are we investing in?"

The objective is to identify the current market climate and monitor systemic risk conditions.

---

# Role Within Orion

Aurora is an observation framework.

Aurora does not generate investment recommendations.

Aurora does not execute trades.

Aurora provides context for understanding market conditions.

---

# Core Functions

Aurora is responsible for:

* Market regime identification
* Risk monitoring
* Liquidity monitoring
* Trend monitoring
* Macro environment assessment

---

# Output States

Aurora classifies the investment environment into three primary states.

## Risk On

Characteristics:

* Strong market participation
* Favorable liquidity conditions
* Positive trend conditions
* Low systemic stress

---

## Neutral

Characteristics:

* Mixed signals
* Uncertain trend conditions
* Moderate risk environment

---

## Risk Off

Characteristics:

* Elevated market stress
* Weak participation
* Deteriorating trend conditions
* Defensive environment

---

# Scoring Framework

Aurora should ultimately produce a standardized score.

Range:

0 – 100

Interpretation:

* 90 – 100 Exceptional
* 80 – 89 Strong
* 70 – 79 Healthy
* 60 – 69 Stable
* 50 – 59 Neutral
* 40 – 49 Weak
* 30 – 39 Danger
* 0 – 29 Critical

This framework aligns with the common Orion scoring model.

---

# Relationship With Other Frameworks

Moon:

Uses market signals for tactical allocation.

Supernova:

Uses long-term megatrend analysis.

Phoenix:

Uses digital asset ecosystem analysis.

Aurora:

Provides environmental context.

Aurora does not directly override the outputs of other frameworks.
