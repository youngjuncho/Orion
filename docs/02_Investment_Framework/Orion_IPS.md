# Orion Investment Policy Statement (IPS)

## Purpose

This document defines the investment philosophy, portfolio structure, governance rules, and operational principles of Orion OS.

Orion OS is designed to support systematic investment decisions through observable market states rather than predictions.

The objective is long-term capital growth through a combination of tactical asset allocation, megatrend investing, and digital asset exposure.

---

# Investment Philosophy

## Core Principles

### Observe More, Predict Less

Investment decisions should be driven by observable data rather than forecasts.

### Systems Over Opinions

Repeatable processes are preferred over discretionary judgment.

### Signals Over Information

The objective is not information consumption but signal extraction.

### State Over News

Market state is more important than daily news flow.

---

# Portfolio Architecture

Orion consists of four independent investment frameworks.

## Moon

Tactical Asset Allocation Framework

Purpose:

* Capital preservation
* Risk management
* Tactical asset allocation

Characteristics:

* Monthly rebalancing
* Rules-based
* Research-driven

Primary Question:

"What should be held right now?"

---

## Aurora

Market Climate Framework

Purpose:

* Identify current market regime
* Monitor macroeconomic conditions
* Measure systemic risk

Primary Question:

"What environment are we investing in?"

Outputs:

* Risk On
* Neutral
* Risk Off

---

## Supernova

Megatrend Equity Framework

Purpose:

* Long-term accumulation of dominant companies
* Exposure to structural economic trends
* Monitoring of moat durability

Primary Question:

"Which companies are most likely to dominate the future?"

Framework:

5D Megatrends

* Decoupling
* Deglobalization
* Demographics
* Decarbonization
* Digital Transformation

---

## Phoenix

Digital Asset Framework

Purpose:

* Monitor category-leading digital assets
* Identify emerging ecosystem leaders
* Provide exposure to next-generation digital infrastructure

Primary Question:

"Which digital assets are becoming category leaders?"

---

# Moon Policy

## Strategy Selection

Moon does not create proprietary investment strategies.

Only academically researched or publicly validated strategies may be implemented.

Approved Strategies:

* ADM
* BAA
* BDA
* HAA
* VAA

Implementation must follow original methodology as closely as possible.

---

## Rebalancing Policy

Frequency:

Monthly

Execution Window:

Last trading day of each month

Objective:

Maintain alignment with selected strategy signals.

---

# Aurora Policy

Aurora does not generate investment recommendations.

Aurora provides environmental context.

Outputs are informational and intended to support decision making.

Aurora is the market climate layer of Orion OS.

---

# Supernova Policy

## Objective

Build a concentrated portfolio of dominant companies benefiting from long-term structural trends.

---

## Selection Criteria

Companies must satisfy the following conditions:

1. Direct exposure to at least one 5D megatrend
2. Durable competitive advantage
3. Strong market position
4. Long-term growth potential
5. Strategic relevance within future economic infrastructure

---

## Accumulation Policy

Investment Frequency:

Monthly

Method:

Dollar Cost Averaging

---

## Replacement Policy

Holdings are not replaced due to:

* Short-term price declines
* Temporary volatility
* Valuation fluctuations

Holdings may be replaced only when:

* The approved company list changes
* A superior category leader emerges
* Competitive advantage materially deteriorates
