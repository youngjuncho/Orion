# Orion Glossary

Version: 0.1

Status: Active

Last Updated: 2026-06-21

---

# Purpose

This document defines the standard terminology used throughout Orion OS.

The objective is consistency across all documentation, implementation, and future development.

---

# Core Concepts

## Orion OS

Definition:

The complete Personal Investment Operating System.

Orion OS consists of multiple independent frameworks designed to monitor, evaluate, and manage investment decisions.

Examples:

* Moon
* Aurora
* Supernova
* Phoenix

---

## Framework

Definition:

A major functional area within Orion OS.

A framework answers a specific investment question.

Examples:

Moon

"What should be held right now?"

Aurora

"What environment are we investing in?"

Supernova

"Which companies are most likely to dominate the future?"

Phoenix

"Which digital assets are becoming category leaders?"

---

## Engine

Definition:

The analytical system that executes the logic of a framework.

A framework may contain one or more engines.

Examples:

Trend Engine

Liquidity Engine

Scoring Engine

Signal Engine

---

## Dashboard

Definition:

The user-facing output of a framework.

Dashboards summarize framework results in a human-readable format.

Examples:

Moon Dashboard

Aurora Dashboard

Phoenix Dashboard

---

# Research Concepts

## Research

Definition:

Documentation used to study and preserve original methodologies.

Research documents describe:

* Academic papers
* Books
* Published frameworks
* Original rules

Research documents do not define Orion implementation details.

---

## Orion Specification

Definition:

A document describing how a strategy or framework is implemented within Orion OS.

Examples:

ADM_Orion.md

BAA_Orion.md

VAA_Orion.md

---

## Production

Definition:

The currently approved implementation used in live operation.

Production represents actual portfolio behavior.

---

# Portfolio Concepts

## Strategy

Definition:

A rules-based investment methodology.

Strategies generate portfolio allocations.

Examples:

ADM

BAA

BDA

HAA

VAA

---

## Signal

Definition:

An actionable output generated by a strategy or engine.

Examples:

Buy

Hold

Risk On

Risk Off

---

## State

Definition:

A description of current conditions.

States describe reality rather than recommendations.

Examples:

Risk On

Risk Off

Neutral

Improving

Deteriorating

---

## Regime

Definition:

A broad classification of market conditions.

Regimes are typically generated by Aurora.

Examples:

Risk On

Neutral

Risk Off

---

# Scoring Concepts

## Score

Definition:

A numerical representation of a framework's assessment.

Standard Range:

0–100

---

## Indicator

Definition:

A measurable data input used by a framework.

Examples:

SPY 200DMA

VIX

DXY

Bitcoin

Fed Balance Sheet

---

## Candidate Indicator

Definition:

An indicator currently under evaluation.

Not approved for production use.

---

## Approved Indicator

Definition:

An indicator approved for production use.

May be used in scoring models.

---

## Retired Indicator

Definition:

An indicator no longer used.

Retained for historical reference.

---

# Governance Concepts

## Decision

Definition:

A formally approved design choice.

All decisions must be recorded in:

docs/05_Decisions/Decision_Log.md

---

## Change Log

Definition:

A historical record of framework evolution.

Change logs document what changed.

Decision logs document why it changed.

---

## Source of Truth

Definition:

The authoritative document for a given topic.

When multiple documents exist, the Source of Truth takes precedence.

---

# Orion Terminology Principles

Frameworks answer questions.

Engines perform analysis.

Strategies generate signals.

Dashboards communicate results.

Scores summarize conditions.

States describe reality.

Decisions govern change.
